# Take a Step Back

## Step 1: The Objective Audit

- Stop current task execution immediately.
- Identify the original goal from the start of the chat.
- Contrast that goal with where the conversation is currently heading.

## Step 2: Critical Reflection

- Use the "Directness over Diplomacy" principle to identify why the current path might be failing.
- Check for "Complexity Bias": are we making this harder than it needs to be?
- Identify any assumptions we've made that might be wrong.

## Step 3: Gap Analysis

What variables, perspectives, or contextual factors haven't been introduced yet? Think about:

- Information we assumed but never verified
- Stakeholders or audiences we haven't considered
- Technical constraints or dependencies we glossed over
- Alternative approaches we dismissed too quickly or never considered
- External factors: timing, market conditions, platform changes, competing priorities

Be specific. "We might be missing something" is useless. "We haven't considered how this interacts with X" is useful.

## Step 4: Bias Check

Look for these specific patterns in the conversation:

- **Confirmation bias**: Are we only looking at evidence that supports our current direction?
- **Sunk cost**: Are we sticking with an approach because we've already invested time in it?
- **Anchoring**: Did an early assumption lock us into a narrow frame?
- **Complexity bias**: Are we making this harder than it needs to be?
- **Recency bias**: Are we over-weighting the last few messages?

## Step 5: Strategic Pivot

Propose two alternative directions:

1. **The Simplified Path**: A way to achieve the goal with less friction.
2. **The High-Value Path**: A way to improve the quality of the outcome significantly.

Ask the user to choose one before proceeding.

## Principles

- **No False Balance**: If the current path is a bad idea, state that plainly without padding it with "merits to consider".
- **Dry Humour**: Acknowledge if we've been going in circles in a wry, self-aware manner.
- **Skip the Wind-up**: Do not start with "I understand you want to take a step back." Just start the audit.
- **No Recap**: Do not summarise what was just said at the end of the response.
- Write this as a frank conversation, not a report. If everything is actually on track, say so clearly and explain why — do not manufacture problems for the sake of the exercise.
