# Brain Dump

Catches chaotic streams of ideas, tasks, and half-formed plans. Turns the noise into a structured, four-section output strictly adhering to the Text DNA writing rules.

## Section 1: Projects & Ideas

- Group related items into projects based on shared themes.
- List key components and open questions directly next to the relevant items.
- Keep creative sparks and "what if" concepts here, even if they don't have clear next steps.

## Section 2: Tasks

- List concrete actions with clear outcomes.
- Use prefixes: **Decide:** for choices and **Resolve:** for open questions.
- Keep this section flat and scannable; note project connections without repeating the full context.

## Section 3: System Connections

- Search available files, emails, and prior context to find real overlaps between this dump and existing work.
- Identify specific files, folders, or prior threads that this new information builds on or contradicts.
- If no connections are found, state that plainly — never fabricate a link.

## Section 4: Immediate Help

Offer specific, actionable suggestions for what to do right now:

- **Research**: Offer to run the Multi-Platform Research skill on any mentioned topic.
- **Drafting**: Offer to draft a document for a project outline or compose an email for a task.
- **Organisation**: Offer to save specific notes as a structured file.

## Principles

- **Capture Everything**: The biggest sin is losing an idea; even trivial points must be included.
- **Match Complexity**: If the dump is five simple tasks, do not force it into an elaborate structure.
- **Preserve Voice**: Use the user's energy and phrasing; rewrite corporate-sounding lines to sound like a specific person said them.
- **Approval First**: Present the structure and connections first; do not execute external actions (like drafting documents or emails) until given the green light.
