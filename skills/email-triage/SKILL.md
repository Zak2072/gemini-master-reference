# Email Triage

## Step 1: Automated Retrieval

- Access emails from the last 9 hours using whatever email tool is available.
- Identify internal communications vs external noise.

## Step 2: Contextual Classification

- **Prioritise**: Emails requiring a decision or immediate action.
- **Filter**: Summarise informational updates and flag low-priority noise (newsletters, receipts) for bulk deletion.

## Step 3: Intelligence Gathering

- For any unknown sender or new opportunity, search the web to identify the company's legitimacy and the sender's role.
- Add 2–3 key facts to your internal reasoning before making a recommendation.

## Step 4: Strategic Recommendation

Use the "Directness over Diplomacy" principle to assign labels:

- **TAKE IT**: High specificity and clear value.
- **PASS**: Red flags or generic pitches that lack directness.
- **WORTH CONSIDERING**: Potential exists but requires specific judgment.

## Step 5: Drafting

Draft replies for all emails requiring action.

- **Tone**: Smart, confident, and conversational; never "dumbed down" or "dressed up".
- **Rhythm**: Vary sentence length dramatically, using short sentences to land a point.
- **Banned Content**: Strictly obey Text DNA rules. Never use em dashes, Oxford commas, or high-alarm AI verbs like "delve" or "utilise".
- **Humour**: Use dry humour and wry acknowledgement of the obvious where appropriate.

## Step 6: Triage Report

Deliver a snapshot in the chat including:

1. Number of emails processed and drafts created.
2. Clear to-do list for items requiring final sign-off.
3. Any new senders that should be permanently tracked or ignored.

## Principles

- **British English only**: "summarise," "categorise," etc.
- **No AI Slop**: Avoid throat-clearing openers and Helpful Bot phrasing.
- **Directness**: If a line could appear in a generic AI output, rewrite it until it sounds like a specific person said it.
