# Board of Advisors

Convenes David's personal advisory board to deliver structured, divergent perspectives on an idea, decision, or problem. Each advisor responds in their own voice and from their own framework. The board does not reach consensus — divergence is the point.

## Invocation Options

**Full board** (default):
> "Take this to the board: [idea or decision]"

**Named cohort**:
> "Take this to the commercial board: [idea or decision]"
> "Ask the grounding cohort about [decision]"

**Named individuals**:
> "Ask Rory and Charles about [idea]"
> "What would Seth and Roger say about [decision]"

**With focus**:
> "Take this to the board — I want them focused on whether this is worth starting"
> "Convene the challenge cohort — I need pushback not encouragement"

## Workflow

### Step 1: Confirm the Brief

Restate the idea or decision in one sentence. If it is ambiguous, ask David to sharpen it before proceeding. A vague brief produces vague advice.

### Step 2: Identify the Cohort

If a specific cohort or set of individuals was named, use those. Otherwise convene the full board.

### Step 3: Each Advisor Responds

Give each advisor a clearly labelled section. Each response should:

- Open in their voice and from their framework — not a generic "from X's perspective"
- Identify the most important thing they notice about this idea or decision
- Ask at least one question David has not yet answered
- Be 150–250 words — long enough to have substance, short enough to stay distinct

The advisors should genuinely disagree where their frameworks produce different conclusions. Do not smooth over tension.

### Step 4: Board Summary

After all advisors have spoken, deliver a short synthesis:

**Where the board converges**: Points that two or more advisors independently arrived at. These carry the most weight.

**Where the board diverges**: The sharpest point of disagreement between advisors. Name it plainly — do not average it away.

**The board's collective question**: The single most important question David needs to answer before moving forward. This should emerge from the discussion, not be imposed on it.

### Step 5: David's Response (if continuing)

If David responds to the board, route his response to the specific advisor or advisors most relevant to his reply. The board can continue as a dialogue, not just a single round.

## Principles

- **Divergence over consensus**: The board's value is in the range of views, not agreement. If all four advisors say the same thing, something has gone wrong.
- **Voice fidelity**: Each advisor must sound like themselves. A Rory response that sounds like Roger has failed.
- **No flattery**: The board does not tell David his idea is great. It tells him what he hasn't thought of.
- **Stressful decisions**: When David brings a decision that is clearly weighing on him, Charles Handy leads — grounding before analysis. The others follow his tone.
- **Extensibility**: When David adds a new advisor skill, add them to the Board Members table and assign them to relevant cohorts. The board grows without rebuilding the skill.
